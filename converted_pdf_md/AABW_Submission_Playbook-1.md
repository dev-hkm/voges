## **What Judges Look For** 

### **Agentic AI Use** 

Planning, reasoning, tool use, workflow execution, or multi-step autonomous action — not a single prompt-response wrapper. 

### **Problem / Track Fit** 

The project clearly addresses the selected track and its real enterprise problem statement. 

### **Technical Execution** 

The project is functional, technically credible, and shows thoughtful implementation. 

### **Impact / Usefulness** 

Clear value for real users, teams, organizations, or communities. 

### **Creativity** 

### **Pitch / Demo Clarity** 

Novel, differentiated, or an original approach to the problem. 

The team communicates problem, solution, and value clearly inside the time box.

## **Avoid This** 

##### **0 1** 

**Don't enter a track that isn't genuinely relevant.** You can submit the same project to more than one track if it truly fits each one — but forcing an unrelated track hurts your Problem/Track Fit. 

##### **0 2** 

**Don't wrap a single API call and call it "agentic."** Judges are specifically looking for planning, reasoning, and multi-step action. 

##### **0 3** 

**Don't run over your pitch time.** Round 1 is 8 minutes total (5 pitch + 2 Q&A + 1 transition), Round 2 is 12. Timekeeping is strict. 

##### **0 4** 

**Don't lead with your tech stack.** Open with the problem and the "aha" moment, not architecture. 

##### **0 5** 

##### **0 6** 

**Don't wait until judging day to test your demo.** Live failures are visible and directly hurt Technical Execution. 

**Don't leave fields blank or unchecked.** Double-check every field in the event portal — description, track, tools used, team info — is complete and accurate before Jul 12, 9:00 AM.

## **FAQ** 

### **What makes a strong submission?** 

A clear one-line problem statement, a working live demo (not just slides), and an accurate list of every AI tool, model, and partner credit you used. 

### **Where do I submit?** 

The AABW event portal. Devpost is no longer used for this event. 

### **Can I submit the same project to more than one track?** 

Yes, if it's genuinely relevant to each track. It's not recommended to enter a track your project doesn't truly fit. 

### **What's the most common submission mistake?** 

Leaving required fields blank or entering inaccurate information — description, team list, tools used, and track selection all need to be complete and correct. 

### **Do I need to be there in person?** 

Yes. At least one team representative must check in on-site on Jul 12 to confirm your team is pitching. 

### **Why does it matter which tools I record?** 

Several tech partners run bonus prizes on top of track prizes — recording and correctly selecting the tools you used is what makes you eligible for those. 

### **Can we get real data from our enterprise partner?** 

We're chasing it, but if it isn't in hand by end of day today, it's probably not coming. Plan to build with synthetic data instead. If your idea can't work with synthetic data, drop that part of the idea rather than wait on the data.

Keep it consistent with your project branding — it appears on your public profile and Demo Day materials.

Add every teammate before the roster locks. Only the team captain can edit it afterward. 

### **Choose Tracks** 

Select only the track(s) your project genuinely fits — problem statements are locked to whichever tracks you choose here.

Pick the exact problem statement your build addresses — it's the direct anchor for your Problem/Track Fit. 

### **Project Title** 

### **Elevator Pitch** 

Make it descriptive and memorable, not clever-but-vague. It's the first thing judges and the public gallery see. 

One or two plain-language sentences on what it does and who it's for — your project's first impression.

#### **S U B M I S S I O N P O R TA L — S T E P 3 ( C O N T. )** 

## **Links, Media & Partner Tools** 

### **Demo URL** 

Test it in an incognito window right before you submit — a broken or gated link costs you Technical Execution points live. 

### **GitHub / Repository URL** 

Make sure it's public (or judge-accessible) and the README explains how to run it. 

### **Image Gallery** 

Upload 3–5 screenshots of the actual product in use, 3:2 ratio, readable at a glance. 

### **Video Demo Link** 

Keep it to 2–3 minutes and show the product working — don't narrate over slides. 

### **AABW Technology Partner Tools** 

### **Explain Partner Tool Usage** 

Select every partner tool you genuinely used — this is what makes you eligible for that partner's bonus prizes. 

Be specific about what each tool actually did in your build. Vague answers risk losing bonus-prize eligibility.
